## Emotional Frame

The teacher's core pain is not incompetence — it's time. They know their children deeply. They care. But the administrative layer around assessment — observing, documenting, drafting reports, updating trackers — consumes hours that should go to teaching. 45% of early childhood educators reported battling burnout in a 2024 survey, with administrative load as a leading cause.

The copy must speak directly to this: Nurture gives teachers their time back, not by replacing their professional judgment, but by removing the blank-page burden. Every line should feel like relief, not like a pitch.

## Section 8: Q&A

**Q1: How does Nurture track milestone progress without me doing the work?**
When a child completes an activity, Nurture records the result and updates their milestone tracker automatically. For skill-based milestones (Language & Literacy and Numeracy), three consecutive passes — or five out of seven — counts as mastery. For Social & Emotional milestones, you log a quick observation on the spot, and five separate-day observations marks the milestone achieved. The system does the counting; you do the observing.

**Q2: Does this replace my professional judgment?**
No. Nurture generates activity queues and report drafts based on data — but you review, adjust, and approve everything. Activity queues can be swapped. Report drafts can be rewritten. The system handles the blank-page problem; your professional knowledge handles everything else. You remain essential.

**Q3: How long does it take to get set up?**
Most teachers are up and running within one school day. Your class roster, learning areas, and milestone framework are pre-loaded — aligned to the NEL Framework by default. You add your children, and the system is ready to go.

**Q4: What if a child completes activities at home? Does that count?**
Yes. Activity completions from the parent app feed into the same milestone tracker as school sessions. The result appears in your dashboard with a "home" tag so you know where it happened. One continuous record, regardless of where learning takes place.

**Q5: Is Nurture aligned to My First Skool's PETAL approach?**
Nurture's milestone framework is built on the NEL Framework, which is the foundation of the PETAL pedagogical approach. The two are compatible — no separate milestone schema or additional configuration is required.
