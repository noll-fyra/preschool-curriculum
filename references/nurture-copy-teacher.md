## Emotional Frame

The teacher's core pain is not incompetence — it's time. They know their children deeply. They care. But the administrative layer around assessment — observing, documenting, drafting reports, tracking attendance, managing parent messages — consumes hours that should go to teaching. 45% of early childhood educators reported battling burnout in a 2024 survey, with administrative load as a leading cause.

The copy must speak directly to this: Nurture is a **morning briefing, not a control panel.** A teacher opens it at 7:45am with ten minutes before children arrive. It answers three questions instantly, without navigation:

1. **Who's here today?**
2. **What are we doing?**
3. **Who needs my attention?**

Every line should feel like relief, not like a pitch.

## Section 8: Q&A

**Q1: What does the morning dashboard actually show?**
Four stat cards at the top: children present, activities today, observations logged this week, and children flagged for attention. Below that, every child in the class as a card — with a presence indicator (present, absent, late) and five domain coverage dots showing which learning areas have been observed this week. On the right: one AI insight and your unread parent messages. Everything you need, before the first child walks in.

**Q2: How does Quick-Log work?**
Tap the button from anywhere in the app — it's always one tap away. Select the child from the grid or type their name. Speak or type a one-line observation note. The system suggests a domain tag in real time. Confirm it, or override. Tap save. The sheet dismisses immediately. Most teachers complete the whole flow in under 20 seconds.

**Q3: Does this replace my professional judgment?**
No. Nurture generates AI insights, domain suggestions, and report drafts based on data — but you review, adjust, and approve everything. Insights are dismissible. Domain tags can be overridden. Report drafts can be rewritten. The system handles the blank-page problem; your professional knowledge handles everything else. You remain essential.

**Q4: How does Nurture know which children need my attention?**
The system flags children when: there's been no observation in any domain for 5+ days, a specific domain has had no observation for 10+ days, there's an unread parent message relating to that child, or you've manually flagged them. The flag appears on their card in the children grid. The stat strip at the top of the dashboard always shows the count.

**Q5: Is Nurture aligned to My First Skool's PETAL approach?**
Nurture's curriculum framework is built on the NEL Framework, which is the foundation of the PETAL pedagogical approach. All five domains — including Motor and Creative Arts — are covered. No separate configuration or domain mapping is required.
