# Nurture — Landing Page Copy: Parents
*Persona: Parents and guardians of preschool children aged 3–6*

---

## Emotional Frame

Parents of preschool children live with a specific anxiety: their child has entered an institution — school — for the first time, and the parent has almost no visibility into what's happening there. A once-a-term progress report doesn't close this gap. WhatsApp messages from teachers are warm but informal. Parents want to be informed, not just reassured.

The two distinct parent personas from the PRD must both feel served:
- **Passive parents** (~50%): want their child to be able to learn independently. They don't always have time to sit alongside their child, but they want to know their child is progressing and doing the right things.
- **Active parents** (~50%): want to participate. They want co-activities, context, and suggestions for what to do together.

The copy must speak to both without alienating either. The framing: Nurture meets you where you are. Whether you have ten minutes or an evening, you'll always know how your child is doing — and always have something you can do if you want to.

The parent dashboard is explicitly designed as an "anxiety-management tool that happens to contain data." Every copy choice should echo this: warmth before data, context before numbers, always.

## Section 8: Q&A

**Q1: What does the app actually show me?**
Your home screen shows a plain-language weekly summary, your child's current developmental level across three areas (Language & Literacy, Numeracy, and Social & Emotional Development), and a live feed of recent activity completions and milestone achievements. Each piece of data comes with context — what it means, why it matters, and what's coming next. No raw scores without explanation.

**Q2: How is this different from the updates I already get from school?**
Most schools send formal progress updates once or twice per term. Nurture updates automatically every time your child completes an activity — at school or at home. You'll know about a milestone the day it happens, not three months later at a parent-teacher meeting.

**Q3: Do I have to do activities with my child, or can they do it independently?**
Both work. If you have time to sit alongside your child, Nurture will suggest co-activities you can do together. If you want to hand your child the phone and let them go, the experience is entirely self-guided — Pip speaks every instruction aloud, so your child doesn't need to read anything. You choose what fits.

**Q4: Will I be able to see how my child compares to the rest of the class?**
No — and this is intentional. Nurture never shows class averages, percentile rankings, or any data that implies comparison to other children. Your child's progress is measured only against their own journey. There is no data point in Nurture that could make you feel your child is "behind."

**Q5: Is this app separate from what the school uses?**
No. Nurture is one connected platform. The teacher's dashboard, your parent app, and your child's activity experience are all part of the same system. When your child completes an activity at home, it appears in their teacher's dashboard. When the teacher logs an observation at school, it appears in your feed. One continuous record — wherever learning happens.
